A shiok lesson
 
Shiok
/shee-oak/
 
‘Aiya how to tell non-Singaporeans what is ‘shiok’…’
‘They sure won’t understand one.’
‘Ya lah, if they are not Singaporean, how can they know, right not?’
‘Sure very hard to explain one mah.’
‘Ya lor.'
‘Aiya then you must tell people what is Singlish first mah.’
‘I also know.’
‘I try and try also cannot then how?’
‘Then try harder lor’...

A true blue Singaporean would know ‘shiok’ is how we convey great satisfaction or pleasure in the same spirit of exclamations like Bravo! Fabulous! Wow!

Singaporeans commonly use ‘shiok’ as an adjective to refer to the quality of goods and services, places, things, events and in particular, food!  It is an expression of happiness and pleasure.  Examples are:
  • ‘This plate of prawn noodles is very shiok’. (Very delicious)
  • ‘This mattress is so shiok (extremely comfortable), I can sleep here now.’
  • ‘I love coming here, shopping is so shiok’. (Very eventful) 
We aim to bring some shiok into your life, so enjoy, the newsletter and get ready for the shiok weekend in 3 weeks!
